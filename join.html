Electronic Systems Team

As a software member of KTH Hyperloop you will be developing the software needed for the pod, such as the use and implementation of different communication-protocols such as CAN and SPI, in order to read and control the state of the pod. The work is quite versatile, ranging from designing kalman-filters for precision readings, to designing PCB's and hardware electronics.

Skills needed for this position:

  • Knowledge of electronics, such as EMI, PCB-design and microcontrollers
  • Knowledge in programming, such as C, python, ...
  • Knowledge in Matlab and Simulink
  • Team player
  • Ability to think outside the box, and solve problems.

We expect you to devote at least 10 hours per week. Last, but not least, we are looking for students with a passion to work with us to build the fastest and safest pod ever in Scandinavia.

Are you an Electrical Engineer or interested in Electrical Engineering? Do you want to get chance to get hands-on experience on Powertrain design, manufacturing and testing? Be a part of the Propulsion team and get ready to get your hands dirty.

We are now looking for enthusiastic students, who are willing to devote a full academic year, to take the next step in building Hyperloop technology. Become part of the team and get once in a life time opportunity work on the fastest, most talked about mode of modern transportation and contribute towards sustainable transport. You will get the opportunity to work and innovate on challenging topics like designing a motor, the power systems of the pod and also integrating the propulsion system to our pod.

During this period, you get to work with a multidisciplinary team of motivated students. You get the opportunity to apply the knowledge you gained during your studies or discover something completely new. You get the feel of how it is to work in a challenging project environment. During the foreseeable future, we require students in two different applications:

R&D:
For master students with knowledge in electric motors, we are conducting research on Linear Induction Motors, with the target of publishing a report on the topic in late Autumn. This reseach will, if the results are promising enough, be the basis for an LIM used in one of our pod iterations. The knowledge derived from the publication will also act as a basis for future reserach on linear propulsion.COMSOL as well as MatLab and Simulink will be frequently used during this work, hence why experience in these softwares is meritorious.

Pod integration, assembly and testing:
In the meantime, a simpler propulsion system will be used for our first manufactured pod. For this system to be verified, we need students with a genuine interest in powertrain systems to test the chosen propulsion system, as well as optimise how it will be utilised and integrated in the pod for maximum speed. This role will include more practical work, but also a fair share of analysis. However, it is not as theoretically dense, and as long as you are prepared to learn the ropes by devoting a lot of your time and energy to the project, you are welcome to apply.
